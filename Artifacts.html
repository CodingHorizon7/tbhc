Featured Artifacts

Native American Artifacts

These artifacts showcase the daily life and cultural practices of Native American tribes in the Tampa Bay region. The handcrafted dolls were not just toys but also served as teaching tools, helping children learn about traditions, social roles, and storytelling. The tools, carefully crafted from stone, wood, and other natural materials, highlight the ingenuity and resourcefulness of these communities, used for hunting, crafting, and everyday survival. Together, these objects offer a window into the rich cultural heritage, skills, and lifestyle of the region’s early inhabitants.

Age of Discovery Artifacts

Astrolabes were essential navigational instruments during the Age of Discovery, a transformative period from the 15th to 17th centuries when European explorers embarked on daring voyages to chart unknown lands, seas, and trade routes. These devices enabled sailors to measure the altitude of the sun, moon, and stars, helping them determine their latitude and navigate across vast, often dangerous oceans with remarkable precision. Crafted from durable metals such as brass and adorned with intricate engravings, astrolabes are a testament not only to the scientific innovation of the era but also to its artistic and technical craftsmanship. During this time, nations like Spain, Portugal, England, and the Netherlands competed fiercely for exploration, colonization, and control of new trade routes, and tools like astrolabes played a central role in their successes. Beyond their practical use, astrolabes symbolize the intellectual curiosity, astronomical knowledge, and mathematical skill that defined the period. They also reflect the interconnectedness of science, exploration, and culture, as knowledge from the Islamic world, classical Europe, and other regions was combined and improved upon to advance navigation. These artifacts offer a window into an age when courage, curiosity, and human ingenuity reshaped the world, leading to the establishment of global trade networks, cultural exchanges, and discoveries that forever changed the course of history.

17th Century Artifacts

The 17th-century ship’s bell illustrates life at sea during a period of maritime expansion and international trade. Bells were essential for marking time on ships, regulating sailors’ watches, and signaling important events or emergencies. Made of durable metals like bronze, these bells reflect the technological craftsmanship of the time and the critical role of seafaring in global commerce. The 17th century was marked by exploration, colonization, and the growth of trade networks connecting Europe, the Americas, Africa, and Asia. This bell is not only a practical artifact but also a symbol of the maritime culture, social organization on ships, and the historical significance of trade and travel in shaping modern societies.
