Our Story

The Tampa Bay History Center has been preserving and sharing the rich, diverse history of the Tampa Bay region since its founding in 1989. Housed since 2009 in a 60,000-square-foot, state-of-the-art facility along Tampa’s Riverwalk, the Center is accredited by the American Alliance of Museums and affiliated with the Smithsonian Institution. Its galleries trace more than 12,000 years of history — from Florida’s earliest indigenous peoples through European exploration, and on to Tampa’s growth into a cultural and industrial hub. The Center also preserves one of the world’s most comprehensive collections of Florida cartography through its Touchton Map Library / Florida Center for Cartographic Education, and safeguards an extraordinary archive of more than 90,000 artifacts, documents, and historical materials. Whether exploring prehistoric cultures, early settlers, maritime tales of conquistadors and shipwrecks, or Tampa’s famed cigar-making and immigrant heritage, every visit offers an immersive journey through the stories that shaped Tampa Bay.
