The Sport of Kings and Queens: Florida’s History of Horse Racing

“The Sport of Kings and Queens” traces more than 200 years of horse racing history in the Tampa Bay region — from early 19th‑century contests at Fort Brooke to the founding and growth of Tampa Bay Downs, and beyond.

The exhibit highlights not only the races, but the people — barrier‑breaking jockeys and leaders, including women and trailblazers who shaped the sport both regionally and nationally. It explores how horse racing became woven into Florida’s cultural fabric, offering glimpses into training grounds, historic racetracks, and the evolution of the industry over generations.

Through artifacts, stories, and visuals, visitors can understand how horse racing influenced local economies, social communities, and even national broadcasting — offering a unique window into social, economic, and sporting history tied to Florida and its people.
