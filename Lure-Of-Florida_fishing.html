The Lure of Florida Fishing

Cast a line through more than a century of Florida’s angling history. “The Lure of Florida Fishing” explores how Florida evolved from coastal fishing traditions to becoming a world‑class angling destination. The exhibit runs August 23, 2025 through January 11, 2026.

Inside, visitors encounter over 50 locally made lures, vintage rods and reels, historic photographs, and rare artifacts — each telling a story of anglers, charter captains, and fishermen who shaped the state’s maritime identity. Also featured are fishing maps from the Touchton Map Library, including many once‑guarded “secret spots,” offering a glimpse into fishing practices dating back decades.

The exhibit presents a rich mix of history, art, and storytelling: 22 original fish paintings by Southern artist William Aiken Walker — recovered after years in storage — sit alongside sculptures, carved fish, and memorabilia, celebrating the natural beauty and cultural impact of Florida’s fishing tradition.

“The Lure of Florida Fishing” also highlights how fishing shaped communities, tourism, and environmental awareness, recognizing notable anglers of the past and the conservation efforts that protect Florida’s waters today.
